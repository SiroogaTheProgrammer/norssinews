Teacher-Student Dynamics

By Ronya Sleiman

While observing the classes, my attention was taken by the interactions between the teachers and students. There was a specific patience when the teachers communicated with their students. Simply through their interactions, the mutual respect they had for each other was visible. A great example of this is the ruling on phones during class. I observed four different teachers teach, all with different styles. Yet something they all had in common was their attitude regarding phones during class time. Students freely displayed their phones out on their desks without a reaction from their teachers. On some occasions the student would use it to check the time or message someone.

The moment the teacher believed it was distracting the student from the lesson, a small comment was given (e.g., �*students name* your phone�). And surprisingly, without any complain, groans, or even a single eyeroll, the students would listen immediately. When I asked one of the teachers about this, they replied, �I understand sometimes they need to use a translator.� & �These students are still dealing with the trauma and culture shock of arriving here.� This response meant a lot to me, it showed how mindful the teachers are with the students. A lot of energy isn�t wasted on strict rulings, but rather making the transition to this new place smooth for the students.

Along with all of this, it was particularly rare to see a student not following the lesson. Active listening and participation seemed to be the standard. I understood this standard as the respect given back to the teachers by the students.

Another element I adored about their interactions was good-natured teasing between them. Teachers and students interacted like close friends. Displays of humorous banter (e.g., winking/funny facial expressions to indicate joke, endearing nicknames, inside jokes, etc.) were tremendously common to witness. This was a very heart-warming and eye-opening observation for me.

 

School Atmosphere

By Ronya Sleiman

As previously mentioned, the school�s atmosphere was extremely positive. There was a certain warmth it. I soon noticed that this welcomeness of the environment wasn�t specifically presented for me, but it was integrated into the culture there. The cultural exchange within the school has led to this constructive atmosphere.

Teachers are extremely considerate of students� backgrounds. It is an understatement to say that these students have experienced difficulties in life. Bearing in mind this knowledge, teachers make a great effort to add playful tones to the study atmosphere.

I witnessed teachers make funny comments in the mother tongues of the students, displaying understanding, acceptance, and respect to the diversity in the class. In return, the students exhibited comfortability and security in their environment.

Lessons Learned

By Ronya Sleiman

My two-day visit to AIPE, that I thought would merely provide me with writing material, led to be a spectacular experience leaving me with a fresh outlook on the daily life of students. After my visit, complaining about my studies/school feels arrogant. Students at AIPE deal with learning a new language (for most two; English as well), then studying in said new language, all while juggling adult life in a new country. My visit has shown me resilience of the students and the sympathy of the teachers. I believe the cultural diversity is faced with admiration at AIPE. Students are supported for their differences, encouraged to practice their culture/beliefs, all while simultaneously being taught Finnish culture.

The students I met reminded me that learning has no age limit, and that every classroom is a gateway to new possibilities. When with them, I couldn�t help but think education isn�t just about learning, it�s about belonging, growing, and finding your place in the world. Let us now learn from AIPE, let�s not just tolerate diversity, let�s celebrate it. The more we learn from each other, the richer our communities become.
